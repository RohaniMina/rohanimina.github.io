Go-to-market · Agentic AI

GTM positioning for an agentic AI platform

At Aktus AI, I owned end-to-end positioning for a platform automating expert-grade development reports for an enterprise client in a highly regulated industry. Twelve subject-matter-expert interviews and fourteen annotated expert reports revealed that factual accuracy alone does not create trust; logic clarity, source transparency, and tone alignment do. Those findings shaped the prompt architecture, the product, and the sales narrative together.

Outcome: a skeptical enterprise client became the platform's internal champion.

Research · Developer adoption

How engineering teams adopt developer tools

Embedded in Meta's Developer Experience organization for over a year, I led nine-plus mixed-methods studies on developer tools and AI-assisted workflows across Instagram, WhatsApp, hardware, and infrastructure. I synthesized feedback from thirty-plus product areas into roadmap recommendations for senior leadership, and co-developed an AI-driven text-analysis system later used as a training signal for an internal LLM.

Outcome: adoption barriers surfaced and prioritized; insights informed product investment decisions.

Research · Prioritization

Ranking 25 developer journeys with evidence

At Google, I led a company-wide MaxDiff study across 25 internal developer and employee tool journeys: balanced experimental design, roughly 600 responses, and Hierarchical Bayes modeling to produce individual-level utility scores. The output was a defensible, ranked investment roadmap where every journey had previously seemed equally important.

Outcome: contributed to a 6% improvement in user satisfaction the following quarter.

Enterprise PMM · Technical enablement

Marketing carrier-grade networks at Huawei

My PMM foundation: B2B marketing for TCP/IP network-layer routers powering national telecommunications networks. I met regularly with client executives, ran technical workshops on QoS, MPLS and VPN design, and security for audiences from network engineers to leadership, and built the evidence and documentation that made our value clear before RFPs were submitted.

Outcome: contributed to 15% revenue and market share growth across enterprise product lines.

Multi-audience GTM for a dual-use AI startup

Advising an early-stage AI autonomy startup navigating procurement panels, enterprise buyers, and investors at once. About

The throughline of my career is translation. As a professor, I translated analytics and consumer behavior for students from undergraduates to executives. As a researcher inside Meta and Google, I translated what engineers said into what product teams built. As a product marketer, I translate what AI systems do into reasons people trust them.

I hold a PhD in Marketing and Consumer Behavior from HEC Montréal, where my research focused on trust formation and adoption behavior, the same questions I now work on with AI products. I have taught at Saint Mary's College of California and the University of Saskatchewan, and I came to marketing from an engineering education, which is probably why technical products feel like home.

These days I split my time between building Bellefolio, advising early-stage AI companies, and studying how the newest generation of AI development tools is changing who gets to build software.
